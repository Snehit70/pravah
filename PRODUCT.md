# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Pravah is for an individual planning and executing personal work on a phone.
They often open it briefly, with one hand and divided attention, to capture a
commitment, decide when it belongs, identify what to do next, or confirm that
work was recorded correctly.

## Product Purpose

Pravah turns loose commitments into a trusted flow: Capture, triage in Inbox,
commit work to the Timeline, connect it to Goals, then reflect in Progress.
Success means the user can understand their current commitments and make the
next planning decision quickly without losing context or fearing accidental
changes.

## Brand Personality

Calm, precise, and quietly warm. Pravah should reduce planning anxiety while
feeling deliberate and dependable. Kairo may feel intelligent and distinctive,
but the core product remains a focused planning tool rather than an AI showcase.

## Anti-references

- Not a dense enterprise project-management dashboard.
- Not a playful habit tracker driven by celebration and engagement nudges.
- Not a generic AI-futuristic interface dominated by purple gradients, glow,
  glass, or chat.
- Not a calendar clone with heavy date navigation.
- Not an interface that hides essential actions behind swipe gestures.

## Design Principles

1. Show the next responsible decision. Each surface should foreground the one
   action that resolves its current state.
2. Preserve context. Quick tools use sheets or inline disclosure; sustained
   work earns a full-screen surface.
3. Make state trustworthy. Feedback, labels, and transitions must make every
   mutation understandable and reversible where practical.
4. Prefer visible paths over gesture knowledge. Gestures may accelerate an
   action, but never provide its only path.
5. Keep the tool quieter than the work. Visual style, motion, sound, and Kairo
   support the planning loop instead of competing with it.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Interactive targets should be at least 44 by 44 points, expose complete roles,
labels, values, and expanded or selected state, and remain usable with screen
readers and large text. Color must not be the only state signal. Reduced motion
must suppress nonessential animation. Swipe actions are disabled by default,
sound is opt-in, and haptics can be disabled independently. Copy should remain
clear in compact mobile layouts and allow future English/Hindi mixed text.
